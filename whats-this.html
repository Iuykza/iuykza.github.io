What's This?

Shiftswap is a scheduling application here to help ease the difficulty of swapping shifts between employees. No more digging for people's phone numbers, finding off-days, and determining work preferences. Shiftswap does all that.

How a swap works

Swaps are simple because Shiftswap does all the heavy lifting for you.

  1. Shiftswap pulls schedule data from Kronos.
  2. This data is displayed in a fancy table.
  3. You tell Shiftswap you need a day off.
  4. Shiftswap compares to available employees, their preferences, and sees if they are trained in that role.
  5. Shiftswap advertises your shift through texting and emails.
  6. An employee responds back and says he can do it.
  7. Shiftswap declines any other takers, saying the shift has been filled.
  8. The affected manager is informed of the shift change and gets a final say.
  9. Your shift was approved and it informs you and the trader.

How much effort everyone put in:

Commands

Schedule

today, tommorrow, yesterday
Checks your schedule for this day
mon, tue, wed, thu, ...
Checks your schedule for a day of the week.
prev, next
Shows the previous shift or your next one.
(mm-dd-yyyy), (mm-dd)
Checks your schedule for a specified day. Just use the date format.
Example: 04-15-2016
(time) Lastname Firstname
Checks the schedule of another person. Combine this with any time relevant command above.
Example: tomorrow Joshua Olson.

Clock

in
Shows your clock in for today's shift.
break
Shows your breaks for today's shift.
out
Shows your clock out for today's shift.

Trade

give
Sets up a particular shift for trading.
take
Shows all shifts in trade.
queue
Shows all shifts awaiting approval.

General

help
Shows a list of commands.
remind
delays a response to remind you when you have a shift. You'll get a text that morning, two hours before that shift, and thirty minutes before.
stop
Shiftswap will stop sending you texts. This can be re-enabled on the website or with start.
start
Undo the stop command.
who
Shiftswap identifies itself.
